# The Science of Security: Why Fragment A/B is Unbeatable

Security is about removing single points of failure. In the Fragment A/B model, your data is never stored in one place. **Fragment A** is encrypted and stored in your vault, while **Fragment B** is a physical or digital shard held separately. Even if a hacker breaches Afterglow, they only find encrypted fragments that are useless without the other half. This **Zero-Knowledge** architecture ensures that your privacy is mathematically guaranteed.

## The Single Point of Failure Problem

Traditional security models have a fatal flaw: they create single points of failure. Whether it's a password manager, a hardware wallet, or a cloud storage service, if that one system is compromised, everything is lost.

**Examples of Single Points of Failure**:
- A password manager that stores all your passwords in one encrypted vault
- A hardware wallet that holds all your private keys
- A cloud service that has access to all your files

## How Fragment A/B Eliminates Risk

Afterglow's Fragment A/B architecture splits your access key into two independent parts:

1. **Fragment A**: Stored in your zero-knowledge vault on Afterglow's servers (encrypted)
2. **Fragment B**: Delivered separately via **ShipAny** (physical) or secure digital channels

**Key Security Properties**:
- Neither fragment alone can decrypt your assets
- Both fragments must be combined to access your vault
- Even if Afterglow is compromised, attackers only get encrypted fragments
- Even if Fragment B is intercepted, it's useless without Fragment A

## Zero-Knowledge Architecture

Afterglow uses **zero-knowledge encryption**, which means:

- **We Never See Your Data**: Your master password is used only for local encryption
- **Mathematical Guarantees**: Your privacy is protected by cryptographic proofs
- **No Backdoors**: Even our own engineers cannot access your encrypted vault
- **Client-Side Encryption**: All encryption happens in your browser before data leaves your device

## Real-World Attack Scenarios

**Scenario 1: Afterglow Server Breach**
- Attacker gains access to Afterglow's servers
- They find Fragment A (encrypted)
- Without Fragment B, the encrypted data is useless
- Your assets remain safe

**Scenario 2: Physical Interception**
- Someone intercepts your Fragment B delivery
- They have Fragment B (encrypted shard)
- Without Fragment A, they cannot decrypt anything
- Your assets remain safe

**Scenario 3: Insider Threat**
- A malicious Afterglow employee tries to access your vault
- They can only see encrypted Fragment A
- Without Fragment B, they cannot decrypt your assets
- Your assets remain safe

## Technical Deep Dive

The Fragment A/B system uses advanced cryptographic techniques:

- **Shamir's Secret Sharing**: Mathematical algorithm for splitting secrets
- **AES-256 Encryption**: Military-grade encryption standard
- **PBKDF2 Key Derivation**: Secure password hashing
- **ECDSA Signatures**: Elliptic curve cryptography for authentication

## Best Practices for Maximum Security
